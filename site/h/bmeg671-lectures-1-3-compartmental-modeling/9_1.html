9.1 Why this example matters
The lectures use a butyrate and bone-formation example drawn from the instruc-
tor’s research area. Biologically, butyrate is a short-chain fatty acid produced by
gut microbiota; experimental work such as Tyagi et al. links probiotic-induced
butyrate increases to regulatory T cell expansion, WNT10B signaling, and bone
formation [15]. The classroom task is not to reproduce the full biology. It is to
practice translation:
verbal pathway −→ compartment diagram −→ ODEs.
