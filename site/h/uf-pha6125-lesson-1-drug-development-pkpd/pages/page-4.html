1.1 The two ocial module promises
The module overview is useful because it states the course logic more sharply
than a list of parameter denitions. Module 1A is titled Overview of the drug
development process. Importance of Pharmacokinetics & Pharmacodynamics.
Its promise is that drug development is long, selective, and wasteful: a new
drug may take more than ten years from discovery to approval; many thousands
of candidates can be screened or tested for every approved drug; and a central
development problem is selecting candidates and doses with desirable PK and
PD behavior.
Module 1B is titled Fundamental concepts and basic PK (and PD) parame-
ters. Its promise is more foundational: PK is the traditional language for “what
the body does to drug”, and PD is the language for “what the drug does to the
body”. The lecture then uses clearance, volume, absorption, and empirical PD
models as the vocabulary needed for the later modules.
Put together, the two parts say something stronger than either one says
alone:
Drug development succeeds or fails through quantitative exposure–
response decisions.
Module 1A explains why these decisions matter; Module 1B explains how we
begin to quantify them. This is why the note starts from decisions, then moves
into parameters. A parameter such as CL is not introduced merely because
it is a textbook denition. It is introduced because it controls exposure, and
exposure is one of the few quantitative bridges between dose, patient variability,
response, safety, and the eventual drug label.
