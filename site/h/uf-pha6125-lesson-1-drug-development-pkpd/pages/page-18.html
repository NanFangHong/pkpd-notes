4.5 Dose selection can delay approval
Sacks et al. reviewed rst-cycle failures of new drug applications submitted to
FDA from 2000 to 2012 and found dose-selection uncertainty among preventable
deciencies [14]. This is the regulatory mirror of the scientic problem. If Phase

The practical rule from the lecture is worth preserving:
– Use a wide enough dose range, often at least 10-fold when feasible.
– Prefer more dose levels with fewer subjects per dose when the goal is learn-
ing curve shape.
– Analyze dose response with regression/modeling, not only pairwise tests.
– Separate “the drug has some activity” from “we know the dose for Phase
3.”
