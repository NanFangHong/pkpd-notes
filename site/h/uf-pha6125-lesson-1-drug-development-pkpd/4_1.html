4.1 Why labeling appears in Lesson 1
The rst slide example of prescribing information is not decorative. It tells us
where clinical pharmacology knowledge ends up. FDA’s clinical pharmacology
labeling guidance states that section 12 contains Mechanism of Action, Pharma-
codynamics, and Pharmacokinetics, with Microbiology and Pharmacogenomics
subsections when appropriate [17]. But PK/PD also informs other label sec-
tions:
– Dosage and Administration: dose, interval, titration, maximum dose,
missed dose, formulation.
– Drug Interactions: CYPs, transporters, protein binding, additive PD toxi-
city, antagonism.
– Use in Specic Populations: renal impairment, hepatic impairment, preg-
nancy, lactation, pediatrics, geriatrics.
– Warnings and Precautions: exposure-related toxicity, QT prolongation,
bleeding risk, immune toxicity.
– Clinical Studies: dose arms, endpoint interpretation, subgroup evidence.
