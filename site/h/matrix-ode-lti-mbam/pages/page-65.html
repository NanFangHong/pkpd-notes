16.3 What the plate buys us in toxicology
The GRC toxicology draft’s core idea is that plate notation is a visual grammar
for mechanistic models with uncertainty. In QSP toxicology, this is especially
helpful because the model often combines:
– hierarchical variability across donors, cell lines, animals, or patients;
– latent biological states such as injury stage, pathway activation, or target-
site exposure;
– noisy biomarkers, omics measurements, and histopathology endpoints;
– AI/ML-derived features that summarize images, transcripts, or high-
dimensional assays;
– regulatory decisions that require reproducible model structure, not only
successful prediction.
The plate does not replace the QSP mechanism. It wraps the mechanism in
a probabilistic contract. Once the contract is visible, dierent teams can ask
precise questions: which latent states are inferred, which variables are measured,
which dependencies are assumed, and which modules can be recombined without
changing the meaning of the model.

tation says which uncertainty structure surrounds the QSP map; MBAM asks
which mechanistic directions inside that map are distinguishable for the query.
