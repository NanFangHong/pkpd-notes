40.2 Shrinkage
Empirical Bayes estimates can shrink toward the population mean when individ-
ual data are sparse or noisy. Shrinkage is not inherently bad; it is a property of
borrowing strength. But high shrinkage makes individual ETA-based diagnos-
tics and covariate plots less informative. A model report should state when in-
dividual estimates are data-driven and when they are mostly population-driven.
