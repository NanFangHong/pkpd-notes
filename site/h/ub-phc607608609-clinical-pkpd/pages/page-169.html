40.3 Clinical covariates as imperfect measurements
Creatinine clearance, eGFR, Child–Pugh class, albumin, bilirubin, body weight,
gestational age, and genotype are imperfect measurements of underlying physi-
ology. A covariate model is useful not because the covariate is the mechanism
itself, but because it carries information about the mechanism. Population mod-
eling formalizes this imperfect translation.
