13.1 When elimination becomes a loop
The PHC 608 material on biliary excretion, EHC, and reversible systems lls a
conceptual gap between ordinary clearance and whole-body PBPK. Textbook
elimination arrows often point out of the body. Real systems sometimes return
drug or metabolite to the central pool. Biliary secretion can send drug into the
gut; gut enzymes can regenerate parent from conjugate; reabsorption can create
a second rise in plasma concentration. Metabolism can also be reversible when
parent and metabolite interconvert.

The modeling lesson is that a secondary peak is not automatically assay
noise. It could be meal-triggered gallbladder emptying, enterohepatic cycling,
delayed absorption, formulation release, posture, sampling artifact, or sparse
data. The curve alone does not choose the mechanism. The model should make
testable predictions: timing with meals, fecal recovery, bile diversion, metabolite
prole, or change after transporter/enzyme perturbation.
