18.2 Residual error models
Common residual models include additive,
y = ˆy + ϵ,
proportional,
y = ˆy(1 + ϵ),
and combined forms. On the log scale, multiplicative error becomes additive:
log y = log ˆy + ϵ.
Choosing the residual model changes which deviations are treated as surprising.
This is why residual plots are not decorative; they are direct checks of the
observation model.
