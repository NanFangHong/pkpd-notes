4.3 Observability and identiability are cousins
Observability concerns states. Identiability concerns parameters. They are not
the same, but they often fail for the same structural reason: the output does
not contain enough distinguishable information. In a two-tissue PK model, two
hidden compartments may have exchange rates that are mathematically inter-
changeable if only total plasma concentration is measured. In a viral model,
infected cells and free virions may be hard to separate if only viral load is
observed. The course introduces observability before parameter estimation be-
cause it forces the right question: what can the data possibly know?
