4.4 pH partition is useful, but not enough
The absorption transcript uses aspirin to warn against overreading pH-partition
theory. Weak acids are more unionized in acidic gastric pH, so a naive pH-
partition argument predicts substantial stomach absorption. But real oral ab-
sorption also depends on dissolution, surface area, residence time, unstirred wa-
ter layer, gastric emptying, intestinal permeability, and blood ow. The small
intestine often dominates because it has enormous surface area and longer eec-
tive absorptive opportunity.
The rule is:
pH and pKa tell us ionization; they do not by themselves tell us
absorption.
