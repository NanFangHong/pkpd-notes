Module 2C is titled Drug absorption. Its promise is to explain how orally

Taken together, these three modules form one statement:
The concentration we observe in plasma is the visible trace of input,
distribution, and elimination all acting at once.
This matters because almost every clinical pharmacology decision is an inverse
problem. We give a dose and observe concentration–time data. From that
trace we infer whether a molecule is dissolving, crossing membranes, binding
to proteins, entering tissues, being extracted by liver, ltered or secreted by
