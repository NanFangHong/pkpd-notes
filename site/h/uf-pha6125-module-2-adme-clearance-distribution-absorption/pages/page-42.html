5.4 The Jeong/Jusko challenge to the classical picture
Classical clearance models are teaching masterpieces because they compress
physiology into tractable equations. But Jeong and Jusko point out that the
compression hides assumptions [4]:
– no relevant membrane barrier between vascular blood and eliminating tis-
sue;
– instantaneous unbound plasma–tissue equilibrium;
– intrinsic clearance acts on a concentration that is treated as directly acces-
sible from blood;
– transit-time delay between inlet and outlet is often not represented in closed
form.
Their extended Rattle, Sieve, Tube, and Jar models can be read as modern
counterparts of dispersion, series-compartment, parallel-tube, and well-stirred
ideas. For this course, the philosophical lesson is enough:
Extraction is not only about enzyme capacity and blood ow; it is
also about how drug physically reaches the eliminating site.
This is especially relevant for transporter substrates, polar compounds,
permeability-limited tissues, and PBPK applications.
