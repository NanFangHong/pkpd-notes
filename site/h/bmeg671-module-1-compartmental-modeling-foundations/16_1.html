The rst three BMEG 671 meetings teach a modeling language rather than a

connects hidden dynamics to measurements. Mass balance turns diagrams into
ODEs. Step and impulse inputs distinguish sustained supply from instantaneous
dosing. One-compartment models teach exponential decay, steady state, and
code verication. Two-compartment models teach matrix form, transfer signs,

grammar scales from PK to microbiome-bone biology and infectious disease.
Nonlinear elimination shows why saturation changes qualitative behavior. Veri-
cation checks whether the equations and code match; validation asks whether
the model is adequate for a question. This is the foundation on which the rest
of the course will build controllability, observability, identiability, estimation,
uncertainty, and model selection.
