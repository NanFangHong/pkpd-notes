12.4 Model exchange and SBML
As models grow, reproducibility becomes a real problem. SBML is a community
standard for representing systems biology models in machine-readable form [15].
Students do not need to master SBML in week one, but they should understand
the motivation: a model is more credible when its equations, parameters, units,
compartments, species, and events are represented in a portable, inspectable
form.
