13.3 Warning signs
Warning signs include:
– a model with dozens of states before the student knows what each state
means;
– no available time-course data;
– parameters copied without units;
– a diagram that cannot be translated into equations;
– equations with terms that do not share units;
– a project question that is too broad, such as “model cancer.”
The best project question is narrow enough to simulate and broad enough
to matter:
How does changing this rate or input alter this observable time course?
