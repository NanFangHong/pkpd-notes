12.2 Where NCA becomes fragile
NCA becomes fragile when the question requires hidden mechanisms:
– separating absorption from elimination in ip-op kinetics;
– identifying tissue compartments;
– estimating target-site concentration;
– extrapolating outside observed dose, route, formulation, or population;
– predicting untested regimens under nonlinearity;
– distinguishing low F from high CL after oral dosing;
– modeling time-varying clearance, induction, inhibition, or disease progres-
sion;
– connecting exposure to response with delays, tolerance, turnover, or feed-
back.
That is where compartmental PK, PK/PD, population modeling, physio-
logically based PK, and Bayesian inference enter. NCA summarizes the curve;
mechanistic models explain and predict the curve.
