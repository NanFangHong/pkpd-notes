5.3 Terminal point selection
Terminal point selection is one of the few places in NCA where judgment is both
unavoidable and dangerous. A defensible selection usually asks:
– Are the selected points after absorption and distribution have mostly
ended?
– Do the selected points form a visually plausible straight line on a semi-log
plot?
– Does adding an earlier point bend the line?
– Does removing a late noisy point change λ
z
materially?
– Is the time span long enough to estimate a terminal rate, not just draw a
line through adjacent noise?
– Is λ
