1.4 The two hidden assumptions
The UF lecture names two key assumptions, both about elimination:
(i) drug is eliminated only from the central or accessible pool;
(ii) terminal decline is mono-exponential.
The rst assumption is a statement about where the measured concentra-
tion can stand in for the elimination-driving concentration. If liver or kidney
clears drug from blood or plasma-accessible drug, a plasma curve is a reasonable
summary. If tissue uptake, lysosomal trapping, target-mediated disposition, en-
terohepatic cycling, long tissue residence, or local tissue metabolism dominates,
the plasma tail may be a delayed shadow rather than the actual elimination
process. NCA can still be computed, but interpretation becomes more fragile.
