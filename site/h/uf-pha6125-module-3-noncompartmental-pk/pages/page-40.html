9.2 How nonlinearity appears
Nonlinearity can enter through any Module 2 mechanism:
– saturable absorption or dissolution limits;
– saturable gut or hepatic rst-pass metabolism;
– saturable renal secretion;
– concentration-dependent protein binding;
– target-mediated drug disposition;
– solubility-limited exposure at high doses;
– time-varying induction or inhibition.
NCA detects the footprint before it explains the mechanism. If AUC rises
more than proportionally with dose, clearance may be decreasing, rst pass
may be saturating, or bioavailability may be increasing. If C
