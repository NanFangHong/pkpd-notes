Why full Bayes is not automatically superior
Full Bayesian modeling asks for priors, computation, convergence diagnostics,
posterior predictive checking, and sensitivity analysis. Poor priors can domi-
nate weak data. Poor MCMC can give false condence. A complex Bayesian
model can be less transparent than a simpler ML model if the workow is not
disciplined.
The advantage is conceptual completeness. All uncertainty can be repre-
sented in one posterior. The cost is that every part of the representation must
be defended.
