30.9 Information geometry intuition
Models as approximating families
6.437’s information-geometry view treats a model family as a set of distributions.
The true data-generating distribution may not be inside the family. Estimation
then nds the closest member under a criterion related to likelihood and KL
divergence. This is useful humility for PopPK:
a tted model is an approximation, not the biological truth.
Why misspecication matters
If the residual model is wrong, the closest distribution in the chosen family may
distort structural parameters to compensate. If a covariate is missing, random-
eect variance may inate. If a latent delay is missing, residuals may show time
trend and PD parameters may become uninterpretable. The model can still
converge and produce precise estimates. Precision under the wrong family is
not truth.
Projection language for model building
Model building can be read as changing the approximating family. Adding a
covariate expands the family. Adding inter-occasion variability expands the
family. Changing residual error geometry rotates the family. Adding a mecha-
nistic PD state changes the forward map. Diagnostics tell us where the current
family is failing to approximate the observed distribution.
