30.11 Model reports
What I want from a report
A strong PopPK report should make the probability law recoverable. It should
state:
(i) the structural model;
(ii) the observation model;
(iii) the individual parameter distribution;
(iv) the covariate model;
(v) the algorithm and approximation;
(vi) the diagnostics and predictive checks;
(vii) the decision or scientic claim.
If any item is missing, the reader must infer it, and inference can go wrong.
Parameter table reading
For each xed eect, ask what typical subject it describes. For each random
eect, ask whether it is variance, covariance, or correlation, and whether shrink-
age makes post hoc interpretation fragile. For each residual parameter, ask
on which scale noise is modeled. For each covariate eect, ask whether it is
explanatory, predictive, or causal.
Diagnostics reading
Diagnostic plots should be read as questions:
Observed vs predicted Is the conditional mean structure plausible?
Residual vs time Is there missing dynamics?
Residual vs prediction Is the residual scale right?
VPC Is the population predictive distribution credible?
Bootstrap How stable are parameter estimates?
The plots are not decorations after the model. They are evidence about the
model’s probability law.
