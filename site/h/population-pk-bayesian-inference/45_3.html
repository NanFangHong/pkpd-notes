45.3 What this teaches about EBEs
In this normal model, the posterior mean and posterior mode coincide. In
nonlinear PopPK they generally do not. But the lesson survives: an EBE is
pulled between individual evidence and population structure. The amount of
pull is not moral weakness in the software. It is the consequence of conditional
probability.
The formula also explains why sparse sampling can create misleading post
hoc plots. Suppose many subjects have one noisy sample. Their EBEs will be
strongly shrunk toward µ. A plot of EBE versus covariate may then understate
real heterogeneity or create artifacts depending on how the covariate was already
included in the model. A mature analysis treats EBEs as conditional summaries,
not raw measurements.
