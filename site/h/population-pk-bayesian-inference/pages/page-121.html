32.2 Why clinical asymmetry matters
In some drugs, underdosing is the main danger. In others, toxicity dominates.
For warfarin, both sides matter: too little anticoagulation fails to prevent throm-
bosis; too much increases bleeding risk. A symmetric statistical error metric may
not match this clinical asymmetry. The 6.437 move is to put the asymmetry
into the loss.
