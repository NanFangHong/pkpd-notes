32.3 The danger of decision-blind model selection
Model selection is often performed with OFV, AIC, BIC, diagnostic plots, and
VPC. These are valuable, but they do not automatically answer the decision
question. A model may improve OFV by tting a minor absorption feature
while leaving dose-relevant trough prediction unchanged. Another model may
have similar OFV but better tail calibration for a high-risk subgroup.
The decision-theoretic reading is:
Model adequacy is relative to the downstream action.
This does not excuse sloppy modeling. It prevents a narrow criterion from
masquerading as clinical adequacy.
