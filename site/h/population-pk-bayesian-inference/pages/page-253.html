59.3 My working conclusion
Lavielle’s book feels powerful because it does not treat population modeling as
a bag of tricks. It says: dene the observation model, dene the individual-
parameter distribution, distinguish representation from implementation, and
then choose algorithms according to the task.
6.008/6.437 make that even cleaner. They give the meta-language: prob-
ability law, conditional independence, posterior, risk, marginalization, Monte
Carlo, EM, model selection. Once that language is internalized, Population PK
becomes a special case of a much larger idea:
Model biological variability as latent structure, then infer it from
noisy observations.
