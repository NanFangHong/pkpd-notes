11.4 Approximation is also a modeling choice
6.435 also changes how I read approximate inference. VB, ADVI, MCMC,
importance sampling, SAEM, FOCE, and Laplace are not interchangeable but-
tons. Each gives access to dierent functionals of a hard posterior or marginal
likelihood. So the question is not only
Did the algorithm converge?
but also
Did the approximation preserve the posterior feature needed for the decision?
A variational approximation may give useful posterior means but underestimate
uncertainty; a Laplace approximation may work near a single mode but miss
skewness or multimodality; an MCMC chain may target the right distribution
but mix poorly. In PopPK, these distinctions matter because dose decisions,
covariate claims, and subgroup discovery may depend more on tails and corre-
lations than on posterior means.
