50.2 Why the step size matters
The step size γ
k
controls memory. Early iterations may use larger steps to move
quickly. Later iterations use smaller steps to stabilize. If γ
k
stays too large, the
algorithm remains noisy. If it becomes too small too early, the algorithm may
freeze before reaching a good region.
This is not merely numerical bookkeeping. It is the mechanism by which
Monte Carlo noise is averaged while the parameter estimate moves.
