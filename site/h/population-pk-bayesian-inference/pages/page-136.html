When the individual approach is still useful
The individual approach remains useful for intuition, exploratory plots, and
rich single-subject studies. It can reveal structural dynamics before building
a population model. But when individual proles are sparse, when covariates
matter, or when prediction for new subjects is needed, the population approach
is the natural probability model.
