57.4 Non-Bayesian decision theory
The non-Bayesian decision theory notes help interpret classical operating charac-
teristics: false alarm, detection probability, minimax risk, and constraints. This
matters for pharmacometrics when Bayesian posterior logic is not the only cri-
terion. Regulatory decision-making, trial design, safety monitoring, and model
qualication often ask for frequentist performance properties.
The mature reader can hold both views:
Bayesian: condition on observed data; frequentist: study repeated-use behavior.
